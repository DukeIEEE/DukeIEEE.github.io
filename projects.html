Current Projects

Word Clock

Word clock contains a grid of letters and lights up corresponding letters to form the words that display the current time. The project is currently in its final stages and is ready to awe the world. In addition, the software in the clock can be modified so the clock can display other cool things and even display games. These modifications will be made after the completion of the clock.

Chess Playing Robot

Our senior advisor Tom is spearheading the development of the Chess playing robot. The robot uses a gantry crane system to move its claw on the chess board to pick up and drop game pieces. The project is an iteration of another project Tom plans on making, stayed tuned for that!

BB-8 Robot

Last year Harvey created a simple prototype of the BB-8 Robot that can be remotely controlled. However that prototype was missing the second ball that acted as BB-8's head. This year the BB-8 robot will be completed.

Past Projects

Laser Harp

The Laser Harp was designed as a means of promoting cross-discipline engineering by creating a series of musical instruments that converted readings from the breaking of laser beams into musical notes. This project combined knowledge of Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, and Music Theory in order to design and build a device that many at Duke could enjoy. The ultimate goal of this project was to host an event at Duke in which musicians played our laser instruments in front of an audience.

IEEE Drink Maker

Duke IEEE constructed a drink maker to show off at Duke’s Founders’ Day event in 2014. As expected, the drink maker made drinks, and President Brodhead was the first person to official try it out! The drink maker was controlled using Bluetooth communication from a phone to measure out and mix certain ratios of ingredients to make a fantastic beverage. Also, it had a ton of LEDs that synced up to whatever music it was playing. This thing is awesome.
